About Sailfish Fishing

Interested in hooking the world's fastest fish? The waters east of Kuala Rompin, Malaysia, boast one of the planet's most significant gatherings of sailfish. From March to November, tens of thousands of these sleek predators hunt vast schools of baitfish. With tranquil seas, seasoned crews, and top-notch gear, Rompin has earned its reputation as a premier fishing destination. Numerous charter boats operate in Rompin, making it a globally renowned spot for reeling in sailfish.

Technique

Popping

Hooking a sailfish using the popping technique is undeniably exhilarating, albeit challenging. It entails casting a popper precisely where the sailfish lurks, enticing it to strike.

Watch Video

Sailfish on fly

Catching a sailfish on fly demands a delicate balance of finesse and power. It begins with spotting the telltale signs—a flash of dorsal fin, a flicker of movement beneath the surface. The angler must then carefully select the right fly, mimicking the sailfish's natural prey.

Events

Royal Pahang Billfish International Challenge

The Royal Pahang Billfish International Challenge (RPBIC) is a sport fishing tournament in Pahang, Malaysia that aims to promote conservation of billfish species while also providing an economic boost to the local community. Billfish conservation and maximizing benefits to local community is key for RPBIC continuity apart from continuous support from anglers from local and international.
A three-day of sportfishing and networking event in tropical heaven! 13 - 15 September 2024
International anglers from the around the world compete in Royal Pahang Billfish International Challenge (RPBIC) 2-day fishing event in Kuala Rompin. The winning team will qualify to compete in the Offshore World Championship (OWC) in Costa Rica representing RPBIC as one of qualifying events globally.
